Describe an ML idea you were initially excited about but later decided was wrong or unimportant.

Answer:
At first, I figured the more agents you add, the better the AI system will be. I built an 8-agent pipeline, and found that the costs of coordination, latency, and error propagation often outweigh the gains. Overall, careful decomposition, evaluation and simpler architectures outperformed adding more specialized agents.

How could better accuracy fail to translate into more useful forecasts for a weather model?
Answer:
Better benchmark accuracy does not necessarily mean better decisions. A model might reduce average error but fail to replicate rare but high-impact events, or produce poorly calibrated uncertainty, or issue forecasts too late. For weather, actionable forecasts need not just lower aggregate error, but also timeliness, calibration and reliability.

Describe a time you changed how you use LLMs.
Answer:
I stopped using one frontier model for all tasks. Building my personal AI agent, I was running simple work through Haiku and leaving Sonnet for the reasoning-heavy stuff. This reduced API cost by about 89% while maintaining quality, demonstrating that workflow design was more important than model size.
